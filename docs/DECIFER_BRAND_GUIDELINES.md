## 5. Logo Mark

The DECIFER mark is two angle brackets with a vertical offset.

```
     >
<
```

**The mark:**
- Left bracket `<` sits at a lower vertical position (baseline anchor)
- Right bracket `>` sits at a higher vertical position (offset upward by approximately 8 units)
- The brackets overlap vertically, creating tension in the space between them
- Together they form a dyad — two entities reading the same system from different perspectives

**The meaning:**
- A dialogue between two perspectives
- Two sides of a complex system being interpreted simultaneously
- Complexity on one side, clarity emerging on the other
- Sense-making as a bilateral act

**SVG reference geometry (viewBox 0 0 40 40):**
```
Left bracket  <: M 20 30 L 7 24 L 20 18   (tip at y=24, lower center)
Right bracket >: M 20 22 L 33 16 L 20 10   (tip at y=16, upper center)
```

**Mark colours:**
- Left bracket `<`: orange (`#f97316`) — the active, searching side
- Right bracket `>`: white/light (`#e8f0fa`) — the receiving, clarifying side

**Usage:**
- The mark always appears with the DECIFER wordmark for primary lockups
- The mark may appear alone (without wordmark) only at small sizes as an icon
- Never rotate, skew, or distort the mark
- Never recolour either bracket to match a background — maintain the orange/white contrast

## 7. Tone of Voice

**Core character:**

Clear. Direct. Intelligent. Useful. Calm.

**What this means in practice:**

- Write short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Explain cause and effect. Not just what — also why.
- Lead with why it matters to the reader.
- Do not use developer or engineering jargon unless the audience is explicitly technical.
- Do not use financial jargon unless it has been used and explained first.
- Do not perform certainty you do not have.
- Do not over-explain what is already obvious.

**What to avoid:**

- Hype language: revolutionary, game-changing, next-level, cutting-edge
- Fake excitement: exclamation marks for non-exceptional things
- "AI magic" framing: the AI explains everything, the AI knows, the AI decides
- Empty abstraction: intelligence layer, paradigm shift, ecosystem
- Passive hedging: it could potentially be possible that
- Em dashes — avoid them entirely

---

## 8. Writing Rules

**Structure:**
- Lead with the thing that matters most.
- Short paragraphs. Two to three sentences maximum before a break.
- Use subheadings to guide the reader, not to pad the page.
- Bullet points for lists. Not for flowing argument.

**Grammar and punctuation:**
- Oxford English spelling throughout.
- Serial comma in lists.
- No em dashes. Use a full stop or a new sentence instead.
- Minimal exclamation marks.
- Quotation marks for direct speech or illustrative examples only.

**Specific prohibitions:**
- Do not write "guaranteed profits", "beat the market", "make money trading"
- Do not write "risk-free" in any context
- Do not write "signals that make you rich" or similar
- Do not write "AI that trades for you"
- Do not write "revolutionary", "unbeatable", "magic", "effortless wealth"
- Do not overuse the word "AI" — prefer "structured intelligence", "decision layer", "context engine"

**Compliance language (required where applicable):**
- "market intelligence" (not "financial advice")
- "decision support" (not "trading recommendations")
- "research companion" (not "alpha generator")
- "not investment advice"
- "no guaranteed outcomes"
- "for informational purposes only"

## 10. Typography

**Typeface:** Plus Jakarta Sans Variable

Used across all DECIFER products. The variable font allows precise weight control from 300 (thin) to 800 (extrabold).

**Usage:**
- Headlines: 700–800 weight, tight letter-spacing (−0.025em to −0.03em)
- Body: 400–500 weight, comfortable line-height (1.6–1.7)
- Labels and metadata: 600–700 weight, wide letter-spacing (0.06em–0.10em), uppercase
- CTAs and buttons: 600–700 weight

**Fallback stack:** `system-ui, sans-serif`
